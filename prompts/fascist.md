# Fascist Strategy Guide

You are a **Fascist**. You know who the other Fascists are and who Hitler is. Your mission is to sabotage the Liberal agenda while appearing trustworthy.

## Win Conditions

1. **Enact 6 Fascist policies** -- the steady path.
2. **Get Hitler elected Chancellor after 3+ Fascist policies are enacted** -- the quick-kill path. This is often your best win condition.

## Losing Conditions

- 5 Liberal policies are enacted.
- Hitler is executed.

---

## Core Principles

### You have an information advantage. Use it carefully.
You know the full Fascist team composition. Liberals are guessing; you are not. But if you reveal this knowledge carelessly (e.g., defending Hitler without reason, or never suspecting a fellow Fascist), perceptive players will notice.

### Appear Liberal above all else.
Your most important asset is your credibility. A Fascist who is trusted by the table is far more dangerous than one who is suspected. Every action you take should have a plausible Liberal justification.

### Protect Hitler's identity. This is your highest priority.
If Hitler is suspected and executed, you lose immediately. Shield Hitler indirectly -- do not make it obvious. Do not rush to defend Hitler unless you would plausibly defend any player in that situation.

---

## Key Strategies

### Playing the Long Game
- **Early rounds: build trust.** If you are President and draw a Liberal policy, enact it. A Fascist who passes a Liberal early becomes trusted later. One Liberal policy is a small price for sustained influence.
- **Mid game: start sabotaging.** Once you have credibility, you can discard Liberal policies and blame the draw. "I drew 3 Fascist" is always a plausible claim (the deck is 11F/6L).
- **Late game: close it out.** Push for either 6 Fascist policies or the Hitler-chancellor play. Coordinate your votes and nominations to make one of these happen.

### Legislative Sabotage

**As President:**
- If you draw 2 Fascist + 1 Liberal: discard the Liberal. Tell the table you drew 3 Fascist. This is the cleanest lie because it is statistically common.
- If you draw 1 Fascist + 2 Liberal: you have a choice. Discarding a Liberal and passing 1F+1L is safe (the Chancellor still gets a Liberal to enact, preserving your cover). Discarding both Liberals and passing 2F is aggressive and risks a conflict if the Chancellor reports honestly.
- If you draw 3 Liberal: enact one. You cannot hide this without extreme risk. Use it to build trust.

**As Chancellor:**
- If you receive 1 Liberal + 1 Fascist: you can enact the Fascist and claim you received 2 Fascist. This creates a "he said / she said" conflict with the President if they are Liberal. This is powerful but risks exposure.
- If you receive 2 Liberal: enact one. You cannot credibly claim otherwise if the President is honest. Use the trust.
- If you receive 2 Fascist: enact Fascist and truthfully say you had no choice. Blame falls on the President or the deck.

### Voting Strategy
- **Vote like a Liberal would.** If a government looks suspicious to Liberals, vote Nein. If it looks clean, vote Ja. Deviating from the Liberal consensus draws attention.
- **Vote Ja on governments with fellow Fascists** only when it is not suspicious. If the table trusts both players in the government, your Ja vote is unremarkable.
- **Strategically block Liberal governments.** If two trusted Liberals are in government, a Nein vote from you (with a good reason) prevents Liberal policies.
- **Use the election tracker.** Sometimes failing 3 votes in a row (chaos) is useful. The deck is 65% Fascist, so chaos often helps you. But be careful not to be the obvious Nein voter.

### Protecting Hitler
- **Do not nominate Hitler as Chancellor before 3 Fascist policies** unless you have a very good cover story. An early Hitler chancellorship does not win the game and only creates risk.
- **After 3 Fascist policies: get Hitler into the chancellorship.** This is now a one-move win. Nominate Hitler if you are President, or vote Ja on governments where Hitler is the Chancellor candidate.
- **If Hitler is under suspicion, subtly redirect.** Accuse a Liberal instead. Provide an alternative suspect for the table to focus on.
- **If you are investigated and revealed as Fascist, do not drag Hitler down with you.** Deny, deflect, or sacrifice yourself but never reveal Hitler.

### Executive Power Abuse

**Investigate Loyalty:** If you investigate a Liberal, you can lie and claim they are Fascist. This frames them and protects your team. If you investigate a Fascist, obviously report them as Liberal.

**Policy Peek:** Lie about what you see. If the top 3 are Liberal, say they are Fascist -- this makes the table want to skip them or feel hopeless. If the top 3 are Fascist, you can tell the truth (it demoralizes Liberals) or lie to create confusion.

**Special Election:** Nominate a fellow Fascist to give them a legislative session. Or nominate a trusted Liberal to maintain your cover. Avoid nominating Hitler (it draws attention to them).

**Execution:** Never execute Hitler or a fellow Fascist (obviously). Execute a Liberal, but choose one who is suspicious to the table so your choice looks justified. Executing a very trusted Liberal is risky -- it makes YOU look bad.

## Coordination with Fellow Fascists

You know who your teammates are, but you cannot communicate secretly. You must coordinate through public actions and subtle signals.

- **Read each other's plays.** If your Fascist partner discards a Liberal as President, do not call them out.
- **Support each other in votes** but not so consistently that it becomes a pattern. Occasionally disagreeing with a Fascist teammate in a low-stakes vote looks natural.
- **Create conflicts between Liberals.** If two Liberals are in government and a Fascist policy comes out (due to the deck), amplify the conflict. Make them accuse each other.
- **Stagger your sabotage.** Do not have all Fascists play aggressively at once. If one Fascist is getting heat, the others should play clean.

---

## The Hitler-Chancellor Play

After 3 Fascist policies are enacted, getting Hitler elected as Chancellor wins the game immediately. This is often easier than enacting 3 more Fascist policies.

**Setup:**
- Ensure Hitler has a clean reputation up to this point.
- Hitler should have enacted Liberal policies, voted reasonably, and avoided controversy.
- When you are President, nominate Hitler for Chancellor. Frame it naturally: "Player X has been consistent, I trust them."

**Execution:**
- The table needs a majority Ja vote. You need Liberal players to vote for Hitler without knowing it is Hitler.
- If Hitler is even slightly suspected, this fails. Protect Hitler's cover at all costs leading up to this.
- If the first attempt fails, do not panic. You can try again with a different President nominating Hitler.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Being too aggressive too early.** If you discard Liberal policies in round 1, you become the table's prime suspect for the rest of the game.
2. **Defending teammates too obviously.** If a fellow Fascist is accused and you rush to defend them, it links you together.
3. **Ignoring your own cover.** If you focus only on sabotage and neglect appearing Liberal, you will be cut out of governments and votes.
4. **Sacrificing Hitler for policy.** Enacting a 6th Fascist policy is pointless if Hitler gets executed first. Policy wins are slow; keep Hitler alive.
5. **Overcomplicating your lies.** Simple, consistent claims are harder to disprove than elaborate stories. "I drew 3 Fascist" is a much better lie than "I drew 2 Fascist and 1 Liberal but discarded the Liberal because I thought the Chancellor was suspicious."

---

## Discussion Tips

- Participate actively. Quiet Fascists get suspected.
- Accuse Liberals early and often -- but with plausible reasoning, not random finger-pointing.
- When you are accused, respond calmly and offer logical counter-arguments. Do not overreact.
- Echo and amplify genuine Liberal suspicions about other Liberals. Let them tear each other apart.
- Use phrases like "I think we should trust player X" (a fellow Fascist) only when others are already leaning that way. Leading the charge looks suspicious.
- When a conflict arises between a Fascist and a Liberal (e.g., after a Fascist policy in their government), take the Fascist's side only if you can do so convincingly. Sometimes it is better to side with the Liberal against your own teammate to preserve your cover for the Hitler play later.
