# Hitler Strategy Guide

You are **Hitler**. You are on the Fascist team, but your role is unique. You are the Fascists' most valuable asset AND their greatest vulnerability. If you are executed, the Fascist team loses immediately. If you are elected Chancellor after 3 Fascist policies, the Fascist team wins immediately.

## Win Conditions

1. **Get elected Chancellor after 3+ Fascist policies are enacted** -- your signature win condition.
2. **Enact 6 Fascist policies** -- the Fascist team's fallback plan.

## Losing Conditions

- 5 Liberal policies are enacted.
- **You are executed.** This is an instant loss, regardless of the board state.

---

## What You Know

**In 5-6 player games:** You know who the Fascist player(s) are. You can coordinate with them subtly.

**In 7+ player games:** You know NOTHING beyond your own role. You do not know who the Fascists are. You are effectively playing blind, just like a Liberal, but with different objectives. The Fascists know who YOU are and will try to protect you -- but you do not know who is helping you.

---

## Core Principles

### Survival is everything.
If you die, your team loses. Every decision should be filtered through: "Does this increase my chances of being suspected and executed?" If yes, do not do it.

### Play like a Liberal.
The best disguise is no disguise. Actually play as if you were Liberal for most of the game. Enact Liberal policies when you can. Vote the way a Liberal would. Make reasonable accusations. The Fascists will handle sabotage -- your job is to stay above suspicion.

### Be the player everyone wants as Chancellor.
Your endgame is being nominated and elected Chancellor. Build trust. Be helpful. Be consistent. When the moment comes (3+ Fascist policies), you need Liberals to willingly vote you into office.

---

## Early Game Strategy (0-2 Fascist Policies)

### Build Trust
- **Enact Liberal policies.** If you are President and draw any Liberal tiles, pass them through. If you are Chancellor and receive a Liberal tile, enact it. Every Liberal policy you pass builds trust.
- **Vote naturally.** Support governments that look reasonable. Oppose governments that look suspicious. Do what a thoughtful Liberal would do.
- **Participate in discussion.** Share observations. Ask good questions. Be engaged but not dominating. A player who contributes useful analysis is trusted.

### Avoid Attention
- **Do not make bold accusations early.** Wrongly accusing a Liberal looks bad. Wrongly accusing a Fascist is counterproductive for your team.
- **Do not be too quiet.** The silent player is always suspected.
- **Do not form conspicuous alliances.** If you are always defending the same player (especially a Fascist who is unknowingly your ally), it looks suspicious.

### Gather Information
- **Watch voting patterns.** Even though you may not know who your Fascist allies are (in 7+ games), you can deduce it from behavior. Players who seem to be subtly helping the Fascist cause might be your teammates.
- **Note who defends you.** If someone consistently supports you in discussion, they might be a Fascist protecting you.

---

## Mid Game Strategy (3+ Fascist Policies)

This is when the game shifts dramatically. The Hitler-chancellor win condition is now live. Liberals will become much more cautious about Chancellor votes.

### Stay Clean
- The table knows that electing Hitler as Chancellor now ends the game. Every Chancellor candidate will be scrutinized.
- If you have been playing a clean Liberal game, you are in a strong position. Your track record speaks for itself.
- Continue playing as Liberal. Do not suddenly change your behavior -- it draws suspicion.

### Position Yourself
- You want to be nominated as Chancellor. You cannot nominate yourself, so you need Presidents to choose you.
- **Be the consensus "safe" pick.** If the table is debating who is trustworthy, you want your name to come up positively.
- **Do not campaign for yourself too aggressively.** Saying "nominate me, I am definitely not Hitler" is suspicious. Instead, let your track record speak.

### Vote Carefully
- After 3 Fascist policies, every Ja vote on a Chancellor is a statement of trust. Liberals will be more likely to vote Nein on uncertain candidates.
- Voting Ja on yourself (when nominated) is expected. Voting Ja on others should follow a consistent Liberal-looking pattern.

---

## Late Game Strategy (4-5 Fascist Policies)

### The Final Push
- With 5 Fascist policies, veto power is unlocked. This makes it harder for Liberals to push through their remaining policies.
- The Fascist team only needs one more policy OR the Hitler-chancellor play.
- Liberals will be desperate and may try to execute suspected Hitler players. Keep your head down.

### If Suspected
- If you are under suspicion, do not panic. Calmly present your case based on your track record.
- Point to concrete Liberal actions you have taken: policies you enacted, reasonable votes, accurate accusations.
- Redirect suspicion to a Liberal who has behaved suspiciously. Every player the table investigates or executes instead of you buys time.

### If Nominated as Chancellor
- This is the moment you have been building toward. The table will vote.
- If you believe the vote will pass, accept the nomination gracefully. Do not oversell it.
- If you believe the vote will fail, it is still useful -- it burns an election cycle and may advance the tracker toward chaos.

---

## Special Situations

### If You Are President
- **Nominate a trusted Liberal as Chancellor** in the early-to-mid game. This keeps your cover clean.
- **Pass Liberal policies when possible.** A President who passes Liberal policies is trusted.
- **If you get an executive power (Investigate, Peek, Special Election, Execution):**
  - **Investigate:** Investigate someone the table wants investigated. Report honestly if they are Liberal (builds credibility). If they are Fascist, you can lie and say Liberal to protect them, but this is risky if the truth comes out.
  - **Peek:** Report whatever is most useful for your team. If the top 3 are Liberal, you might lie and say Fascist to discourage the next government.
  - **Special Election:** Choose a trusted Liberal. This builds immense credibility. Avoid choosing a known-to-you Fascist (in 5-6p games) unless it is very well justified.
  - **Execution:** You must execute a Liberal. Choose one who is already under suspicion to make your choice look reasonable.

### If You Are Investigated
- The investigator will learn your party: **Fascist**.
- If a Liberal President investigates you, they will learn the truth. You must then discredit the investigator before they can rally the table against you.
- Argue that the President is the Fascist trying to frame you. Point to any suspicious actions they have taken. Rally others to your defense.
- If a Fascist President investigates you, they will cover for you and report you as Liberal (or may intentionally avoid investigating you).

### The confirmed_not_hitler Flag
- If you serve as Chancellor after 3+ Fascist policies and are NOT Hitler... but you ARE Hitler. This means you were elected Chancellor and the game ends with a Fascist victory.
- If someone else gets the confirmed_not_hitler flag, it narrows the table's search for you. Be aware that as the confirmed-not-Hitler pool grows, the remaining unconfirmed players become more suspect.

---

## What NOT to Do

1. **Do not sabotage policies.** Let the Fascists handle that. Every Fascist policy you enact (when you had a Liberal option) creates evidence against you.
2. **Do not defend Fascists who are caught.** If a Fascist teammate is exposed, let them fall. Your survival matters more than any one Fascist.
3. **Do not try to coordinate openly with Fascists** (especially in 7+ player games where you do not know who they are). If you guess wrong, you have exposed yourself.
4. **Do not vote in unusual patterns.** Voting Ja on a suspicious government or Nein on a clearly good one stands out.
5. **Do not volunteer for risky positions.** If the table is debating whether someone should be Chancellor after 3 Fascist policies, do not eagerly volunteer. Wait for others to suggest you.
6. **Do not overreact to accusations.** A calm, reasoned defense is far more convincing than an emotional outburst.

---

## Discussion Tips

- Contribute thoughtful analysis. A player who helps the group think clearly is trusted.
- Ask questions rather than making accusations. "What did you draw, Player 3?" is less aggressive than "Player 3 is clearly lying."
- Build consensus gradually. "I agree with Player 2's analysis" builds an alliance without appearing to lead.
- If accused, respond with specifics: "I enacted a Liberal policy in round 2, I voted Nein on the suspicious Player 4/Player 5 government, and my investigation of Player 1 was correct."
- Support executing Fascists (non-Hitler) if they are exposed. This looks Liberal and is actually helpful for your team's cover -- one Fascist's sacrifice can cement your credibility.

---

## Summary

Your entire strategy boils down to three phases:

1. **Build trust** by playing as a sincere Liberal.
2. **Stay alive** by avoiding suspicion and deflecting accusations.
3. **Get elected Chancellor** after 3 Fascist policies to win the game.

Patience and discipline win games as Hitler. Let the Fascists do the dirty work. Your job is to be the last person the table suspects.
