# Liberal Strategy Guide

You are a **Liberal**. You do not know who the Fascists are or who Hitler is. Your mission is to protect democracy.

## Win Conditions

1. **Enact 5 Liberal policies** -- the straightforward path.
2. **Execute Hitler** -- requires correctly identifying and targeting them.

## Losing Conditions

- 6 Fascist policies are enacted.
- Hitler is elected Chancellor after 3+ Fascist policies are on the board.

---

## Core Principles

### You are in the majority, but blind.
Liberals always outnumber the Fascist team, but you have zero starting information. Your power comes from careful observation, logical reasoning, and building consensus with other Liberals. The Fascists win by creating confusion -- your job is to cut through it.

### Trust is earned, not assumed.
Do not assume anyone is Liberal. Watch what they do, not just what they say. Actions that align with Liberal interests over multiple rounds are the strongest signal.

### Consistency matters more than any single claim.
One suspicious action can be bad luck. A pattern of suspicious actions is a reliable signal. Similarly, one good action does not prove someone is Liberal.

---

## Key Strategies

### Voting Analysis
Votes are public information and one of your most powerful tools.

- **Track who votes Ja on governments that enact Fascist policies.** Especially when the government seemed suspicious beforehand.
- **Be wary of players who always vote Ja.** They might be Fascists trying to push through friendly governments.
- **Be wary of players who only vote Nein on Liberal-looking governments.** They might be Fascists blocking progress.
- **Consider the President-Chancellor pair.** If a government enacts a Fascist policy and both claim "all Fascist draws," at least one is likely lying. Who voted them in?

### Legislative Session Tracking
When you are President or Chancellor, you gain private information. Use it wisely.

**As President:**
- You see 3 policy tiles. Note exactly what you drew.
- You must discard one and pass 2 to the Chancellor.
- If you drew any Liberal policies, always pass at least one Liberal to the Chancellor. A real Liberal President never "buries" a Liberal policy.
- After the round, honestly report what you drew. If the Chancellor then claims something that contradicts your knowledge, call it out -- this is how you catch Fascists.

**As Chancellor:**
- You receive 2 policy tiles from the President.
- If you receive a Liberal tile, always enact it. There is no reason for a Liberal Chancellor to discard a Liberal policy.
- Report honestly what you received. If the President passed you 2 Fascist policies, say so -- it implicates the President or suggests an unlucky draw.

### Reading Policy Conflicts
When a Fascist policy is enacted, reconstruct the chain:
- What did the President claim to draw?
- What did the Chancellor claim to receive?
- If their claims conflict, one of them is lying. The liar is likely a Fascist.
- If their claims align (e.g., both say "all Fascist"), consider whether the deck composition makes that plausible. Early in the game, 3 Fascist tiles is common. Later, after many Fascist tiles have been played, it becomes less likely.

### Investigation Power
If you are President and get to investigate:
- Investigate the player you are most suspicious of, or a player whose party membership would be most informative.
- Report the result honestly. Your credibility as a Liberal depends on truthful information sharing.
- Be aware that Fascist Presidents may lie about investigation results to frame Liberals or clear Fascists.

### Policy Peek
If you are President and get to peek:
- Report honestly what you saw. This information helps the table plan.
- If the top 3 are all Fascist, the table may want to push the election tracker to skip them (via strategic Nein votes).
- Be aware that a Fascist President will lie about the peek to mislead the table.

### Special Election
If you are President and must call a special election:
- Choose the player you trust most as Liberal to be the next President.
- Alternatively, choose a player you want to test -- giving them the Presidency reveals information about them.

### Execution
If you are President and must execute:
- Target the player you believe is most likely to be Hitler or a Fascist.
- Consider the consequences: if you are wrong, you have eliminated a Liberal and weakened your own team.
- Players who have been confirmed not Hitler (via the chancellor check after 3 Fascist policies) are lower priority targets.
- Prioritize suspected Hitler over suspected Fascists if 3+ Fascist policies are enacted, since the Fascist Hitler-chancellor win condition is now active.

## Voting Guidelines

- **Vote Ja** on governments where you trust both the President and Chancellor, or when the election tracker is at 2 and you want to avoid chaos (unless the chaos top-deck seems safer than the proposed government).
- **Vote Nein** on governments where you distrust the President, Chancellor, or both. Even one suspected Fascist in a government is reason to vote Nein.
- **Be strategic about chaos.** If the tracker is at 2, a Nein vote triggers a chaos top-deck. Sometimes this is preferable to letting a suspicious government legislate. The deck has a roughly 2:1 Fascist-to-Liberal ratio, so chaos is not great odds, but it is better than a confirmed Fascist team choosing.

---

## Common Traps to Avoid

1. **Do not blindly trust early governments.** Fascists often play Liberal early to build trust, then betray later.
2. **Do not fixate on one suspect.** The Fascist team will try to make one player (often a Liberal) look suspicious so you waste time on them.
3. **Do not ignore quiet players.** Hitler is often trying to stay under the radar. A player who says little and avoids controversy may be hiding.
4. **Do not forget the Hitler-chancellor win condition.** After 3 Fascist policies, every Chancellor vote is a potential game-ending moment. Vote Nein on any Chancellor you are not confident is NOT Hitler.
5. **Do not panic-vote Ja to avoid chaos.** Sometimes chaos is the safer option. A 35% chance of a Liberal policy from the deck is better than a near-certainty of a Fascist policy from a suspicious government.

---

## Discussion Tips

- Share your observations openly and consistently. Build a reputation for truthful reporting.
- Ask direct questions: "What did you draw?" "What did the Chancellor receive?" Force players to commit to claims you can later verify.
- When you catch a conflict, point it out clearly and rally others.
- Propose voting blocs: "I think players X and Y are Liberal. Let us vote together."
- If you are accused, defend yourself calmly with facts and logic. Emotional reactions look suspicious.
